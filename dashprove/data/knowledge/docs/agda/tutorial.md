# A List of Tutorials[][2]

Note

Some of the materials linked on this page have been created for older versions of Agda and might no
longer apply directly to the latest release.

## Books on Agda[][3]

* Phil Wadler, Wen Kokke, and Jeremy G. Siek (2019). [Programming Languages Foundations in Agda][4]
* Aaron Stump (2016). [Verified Functional Programming in Agda][5]
* Sandy Maguire (2023). [Certainty by Construction][6]

## Tutorials and lecture notes[][7]

* Martín Escardó and Dan Licata (2022). [Lecture notes for the HoTTEST Summer School][8].
* Danel Ahman and Andrej Bauer (2021). [Logic in Computer Science][9].
* Jesper Cockx (2021). [Programming and Proving in Agda][10]. An introduction to Agda for a general
  audience of functional programmers. It starts from basic knowledge of Haskell and builds up to
  using equational reasoning to formally prove correctness of functional programs.
* effectfully (2020). [Inference in Agda][11].
* Martín Hötzel Escardó. [Introduction to Univalent Foundations of Mathematics with Agda][12].
* Musa Al-hassy (2019). [A slow-paced introduction to reflection in Agda][13].
* Jesper Cockx (2019). [Formalize all the things (in Agda)][14].
* Peter Dybjer (2017). [An Introduction to Programming and Proving in Agda (incomplete draft)][15].
* Conor McBride (2015). [Datatypes of Datatypes][16].
* Jan Malakhovski (2013). [Brutal [Meta]Introduction to Dependent Types in Agda][17].
* Diviánszky Péter (2012). [Agda Tutorial][18].
* Ana Bove, Peter Dybjer, and Ulf Norell (2009). [A Brief Overview of Agda - A Functional Language
  with Dependent Types][19] (in TPHOLs 2009) with an example of reflection. [Code][20].
* Andreas Abel (2009). [An Introduction to Dependent Types and Agda][21]. Lecture notes used in
  teaching functional programming: basic introduction to Agda, Curry-Howard, equality, and
  verification of optimizations like fusion.
* Ulf Norell and James Chapman (2008). [Dependently Typed Programming in Agda][22]. This is aimed at
  functional programmers.
* Ana Bove and Peter Dybjer (2008). [Dependent Types at Work][23]. A gentle introduction including
  logic and proofs of programs.

## Videos on Agda[][24]

* Jesper Cockx (2024). [Programming and Proving in Agda][25]. (Lecture at ZuriHac 2024).
* André Muricy (2023). [Super Haskell: an introduction to Agda][26].
* Andrej Bauer (2022). [Logic in Computer Science][27].
* [HoTTEST Summer School 2022][28].
* Jacques Carette (2022). [What I learned from formalizing Category Theory in Agda][29].
* Fredrik Nordvall Forsberg (2022). [Advanced Functional Programming][30] (videos of lectures).
* Peter Selinger (2021). [Lectures on Agda][31].
* Anders Mörtberg (2021). [Cubical Agda][32].
* Philip Wadler (2019). [(Programming Languages) in Agda = Programming (Languages in Agda)][33]
  (Lecture at YOW! 2019).
* Conor McBride (2014). [Introduction to Dependently Typed Programming using Agda][34]. (videos of
  lectures). [Associated source files, with exercises][35].
* Daniel Licata (2013). [Dependently Typed Programming in Agda][36] (at OPLSS 2013).
* Daniel Peebles (2011). [Introduction to Agda][37]. Video of talk from the January 2011 Boston
  Haskell session at MIT.

## Courses using Agda[][38]

* [Computer Aided Reasoning][39] Material for a 3rd / 4th year course (g53cfr, g54 cfr) at the
  university of Nottingham 2010 by Thorsten Altenkirch
* [Type Theory in Rosario][40] Material for an Agda course in Rosario, Argentina in 2011 by Thorsten
  Altenkirch
* [Software System Design and Implementation][41], undergrad(?) course at the University of New
  South Wales by Manuel Chakravarty.
* [Tüübiteooria / Type Theory][42], graduate course at the University of Tartu by Varmo Vene and
  James Chapman.
* [Advanced Topics in Programming Languages: Dependent Type Systems][43], course at the University
  of Pennsylvania by Stephanie Weirich.
* [Categorical Logic][44], course at the University of Cambridge by Samuel Staton.
* [Dependently typed functional languages][45], master level course at EAFIT University by Andrés
  Sicard-Ramírez.
* [Introduction to Dependently Typed Programming using Agda][46], research level course at the
  University of Edinburgh by Conor McBride.
* [Agda][47], introductory course for master students at ELTE Eötvös Collegium in Budapest by Péter
  Diviánszky and Ambrus Kaposi.
* [Types for Programs and Proofs][48], course at Chalmers University of Technology.
* [Dependently typed metaprogramming (in Agda)][49], Summer (2013) course at the University of
  Cambridge by Conor McBride.
* [Computer-Checked Programs and Proofs][50] (COMP 360-1), Dan Licata, Wesleyan, Fall 2013.
* [Advanced Functional Programming Fall 2013][51] (CS410), Conor McBride, Strathclyde, [notes from
  2015][52], [videos from 2017][53].
* [Inductive and inductive-recursive definitions in Intuitionistic Type Theory][54], lectures by
  Peter Dybjer at the Oregon Programming Languages Summer School 2015.
* [Introduction to Univalent Foundations of Mathematics with Agda][55] , MGS 2019 Martín Hötzel
  Escardó
* [Higher-Dimensional Type Theory][56] (CSCI 8980), courses on homotopy type theory and cubical type
  theory, Favonia, the University of Minnesota, Spring 2020
* [Correct-by-construction Programming in Agda][57], a course at the EUTYPES Summer School ‘19 in
  Ohrid.
* [Lectures on Agda][58], a course by Peter Selinger at Dalhousie University, Winter 2021.
* [HoTTEST Summer School 2022][59], online lectures by assorted instructors.
* [Advanced Programming Paradigms][60], Postgraduate course jointly offered by the Universities of
  Applied Sciences in Switzerland, by Daniel Kröni and Farhad Mehta.
